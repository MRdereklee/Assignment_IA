APU Chess Junior Minor Championship

APU Chess Junior Minor Championship has BEGAN
19 April 2019 | Author: Soo Kok Thong


The APU Chess Junior Minor Championship started on April 16 at 10 a.m. and will include 16 of the grandmasters from different primary school in Malaysia and a prize pool of RM20,000. The winner of the APU Chess Junior Minor Championship will also earn a direct invite to the APU Chess Junior Major Championship and with prize pool of RM50,000.

The tournament is organized by and held in Asia Pacific University (APU).

Each match of the APU Chess Junior Minor Championship will be broadcast live on APU's Youtube Channel and will be hosted by the commentators, Lee Seng Ken, President of APU's Chess Society.

Each match will have the following rules and format:

The winner of each match will proceed to the next bracket. The match will be best of three. The players that lost will be sent to lower bracket. In low bracket, the players had the second chance to fight their way back to upper bracket to continue to become champion.

The APU Chess Junior Minor Championship offers the following prizes:

Round 1: 16 players, 8 matches, RM6,000 prize pool.
Total prizes per match: RM750

Round 2: 8 players, 4 matches, RM4,000 prize pool.
Total prizes per match: RM1,000

Round 3: 4 players, 2 matches, RM3,500 prize pool
Total prizes per match: RM1,750

Round 4: 2 players, 1 match, RM4,000 prize pool
Winner: RM2,000 + direct invite to the APU Chess Junior Major Championship RM2,000 split by win percentage
Total prizes per match: RM4,000
